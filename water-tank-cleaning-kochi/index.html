Kochi's coastal environment and year-round humidity make water tank maintenance more critical than in most Indian cities. The combination of high ambient temperatures, coastal salt air and the biological richness of Kerala's water supply creates conditions where algae, bacteria and sediment accumulate faster than in drier, inland regions. Untreated tanks can harbour E. coli, Legionella and other pathogens that cause gastrointestinal illness — particularly affecting children and elderly residents.

Why Kochi Homes Need Regular Tank Cleaning

Several factors specific to Kochi increase the importance of regular water tank maintenance:

Coastal water quality. Kochi's proximity to the backwaters and Arabian Sea means the municipal water supply carries higher mineral loads and biological content than inland Kerala districts.

Algae in humid climate. Kochi's sustained humidity (often above 80%) combined with occasional sunlight exposure in partially covered tanks creates ideal conditions for algae growth — visible as green or brown discolouration on tank walls.

Monsoon contamination. During heavy monsoon rains, overhead tanks can receive contamination through poorly sealed inlets or overflow points. Post-monsoon cleaning (October–November) is strongly recommended.

How Often Should You Clean?

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) and Kerala Water Authority both recommend cleaning overhead storage tanks at least once every six months. In Kochi's coastal and humid conditions, we recommend:

Overhead tanks: Every 6 months (pre-monsoon and post-monsoon)

Underground sumps: Every 6 months, or whenever water colour or smell changes

High-use residential buildings: Every 3–4 months
