What Is a Sump and Why Does It Need Cleaning?

An underground sump is a large buried water storage tank found in most Bangalore apartments, independent houses and gated communities. It collects water from borewells and municipal supply lines before a pump pushes it up to overhead tanks and into your home. Every litre of water you drink, cook with or bathe in passes through the sump first.

Over time — typically within 3–6 months — sumps accumulate sediment, sludge, algae, insect debris and bacterial biofilm on their floors and walls. This contaminated water then circulates through your entire building's plumbing system. Unlike overhead tanks that are cleaned more commonly, underground sumps are often forgotten — until residents notice discoloured water, unusual taste, or persistent stomach problems.

Signs Your Sump Needs Cleaning Now

  • Unusual smell or taste in tap water — even after boiling
  • Murky, yellow or brownish water from taps or storage containers
  • Visible sediment or particles in water storage vessels
  • More than 6 months since the last professional clean
  • Post-monsoon season (October–November) — rainwater infiltration introduces contaminants
  • New borewell connection or municipal supply change
  • Residents reporting stomach issues or skin irritation

What's Included in Every Sump Clean

Complete Water Extraction

Full drainage of the sump using submersible pumps. We remove all standing water before cleaning begins, ensuring no dilution of cleaning agents.

High-Pressure Wall & Floor Scrubbing

Pressure jet cleaning of all internal surfaces — walls, floor, corners, and inlet/outlet pipes. Removes biofilm, algae, mineral deposits and hardened sediment.

Sludge & Sediment Removal

Manual and mechanical removal of all accumulated sludge from the sump floor. This sludge is the primary breeding ground for bacteria and must be physically removed, not just flushed.

Algae & Biofilm Treatment

Anti-algal treatment applied to sump walls after scrubbing — especially important for sumps receiving borewell water, which promotes algae growth faster than municipal supply.

Food-Grade Chlorine Disinfection

Final disinfection with food-grade sodium hypochlorite solution — the same standard used for municipal water treatment. Safe for drinking water once fully mixed after refilling.

Lid & Inlet Inspection

We inspect sump lid seals and inlet pipe covers for cracks, gaps or damage that allow insects, leaves and surface contaminants to enter. We advise on any repairs needed.

Service Report

After cleaning, we provide a brief report on sump condition, any findings (cracks, seepage, inadequate sealing) and recommended cleaning frequency for your specific setup.

Why Bangalore Sumps Need More Frequent Cleaning

Bangalore's water situation makes sump hygiene more critical than in most Indian cities:

High Hard Water Mineral Content

Bangalore's borewell water is among the hardest in India. Calcium and magnesium deposits form thick scale on sump walls, creating rough surfaces where bacteria colonise more easily.

Heavy Borewell Dependence

Many Bangalore apartments rely primarily on borewell water. Borewell water carries significantly higher silt and bacterial loads than treated municipal supply, leading to faster sump contamination.

Warm Climate Promotes Algae Growth

Bangalore's warm, humid climate accelerates algae and bacterial growth in sumps year-round — unlike cooler cities where biological growth slows in winter.

High-Rise Building Pressures

High-rise apartments generate large daily water demand through multiple sumps. High cycle rates mean faster sediment accumulation and more frequent bacterial recolonisation.
