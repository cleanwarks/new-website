5 Essential Tips for Effective House Cleaning Services in Kochi

Kerala's humidity, dust, and monsoon season create unique challenges for homeowners. These five tips — straight from our Kochi cleaning teams — will help you maintain a healthier, fresher home all year.

1

Start with a Top-to-Bottom Cleaning Sequence

The most common mistake homeowners make is cleaning floors first, then disturbing them again when dusting shelves and fans. Always start from the ceiling — ceiling fans, light fixtures, and shelves — before moving to surfaces and finally the floor. In Kochi's dusty environment, skipping this order means doing the same work twice.

Professional teams at Clean Warks follow a strict room-by-room, top-to-bottom protocol. This structured method saves time and ensures nothing gets missed. Apply it at home: dry dust first, then wipe with a damp cloth, then mop.

2

Address Humidity and Mould Before It Spreads

Kochi's tropical climate means bathrooms, kitchen walls, and window sills are highly susceptible to mould growth, especially during the June–September monsoon. The moment you spot black or green patches, act fast — mould spreads to grout, caulk, and soft furnishings within days.

Use a diluted white vinegar solution (1:1 with water) on tile surfaces and grout. For stubborn patches, a baking soda paste works better than most store-bought products. Ensure proper ventilation — even cracking a window open during and after cooking significantly reduces moisture build-up.

  • Run exhaust fans for at least 20 minutes after showers
  • Wipe down shower walls with a squeegee after each use
  • Check under sinks and behind appliances monthly for hidden damp patches
3

Use Microfibre Cloths Instead of Cotton Rags

Cotton rags push dust and bacteria around surfaces rather than trapping them. Microfibre cloths have millions of tiny fibres that physically grip and lift particles — including allergens — without spreading them. This makes a significant difference in Kochi homes where fine red laterite dust and pollen are constant problems.

Dedicate different coloured microfibre cloths to different areas: one colour for the kitchen, another for bathrooms, another for surfaces. Wash them after every use in warm water without fabric softener (softener clogs the fibres). A set of quality microfibre cloths is the single most impactful tool upgrade for any household.

4

Deep Clean Your Kitchen Exhaust and Ventilation Monthly

Kerala cooking involves a lot of coconut oil, ghee, and high-heat stir-frying — this leaves a layer of sticky, yellowish grease on exhaust filters, tiles, and cabinets faster than in most other cuisines. If left uncleaned, grease attracts insects, produces odours, and becomes a fire hazard.

Remove exhaust fan covers and soak them in hot water mixed with dish soap for 30 minutes monthly. Wipe cabinet fronts with a degreaser weekly. For the stovetop, a warm baking soda and vinegar paste breaks down grease without scratching surfaces. If you cook daily, a fortnightly professional kitchen clean is worth the investment — it takes about 2 hours and removes build-up that home cleaning cannot reach.

5

Schedule a Professional Deep Clean Every Quarter

No matter how thorough your regular cleaning routine is, certain tasks require professional equipment: mattress sanitisation, upholstery extraction, water tank cleaning, and pressure washing of terraces and exteriors. These jobs are physically difficult, require specialised machines, and can actually cause damage if done incorrectly with household tools.

A quarterly deep clean by a trained team covers the areas that accumulate hidden dirt: inside appliances, behind furniture, under bathroom fixtures, and inside air conditioning units. In Kochi, where the combination of heat and humidity accelerates bacterial growth, this isn't a luxury — it's a health measure.

  • Book a deep clean before and after each monsoon season
  • Schedule mattress and sofa cleaning every 6 months
  • Have your water tank cleaned annually (BWSSB recommends at least once a year)

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I clean my house in Kochi's climate?

For most households, a thorough clean weekly and a professional service every 4–6 weeks is ideal. During monsoon season (June–September), mould-prone areas like bathrooms and kitchens should be inspected and wiped down more frequently — at least twice a week.

What's the best way to remove red mud stains from floor tiles?

Kochi's red laterite soil leaves stubborn stains on light-coloured tiles. Act while wet — blot (don't scrub) immediately, then apply a paste of baking soda and water. Let it sit for 10 minutes, then scrub with a stiff brush. For set stains, a diluted oxalic acid solution works well on ceramic, but avoid it on natural stone.
